# V1.17 Self Improvement Expansion - Domain Model

## 1. Overview

The V1.17 Self Improvement Expansion introduces a governed domain responsible
for converting autonomy learning into validated, traceable and controlled
future behavior influence.

The domain extends the autonomy foundation delivered in V1.16 without replacing
existing contracts.

The Self Improvement domain answers:

```text
Which learned improvements are valid, safe and allowed to influence future behavior?
```

It does not execute tools, mutate active execution, alter original data or
replace the responsibilities of Decision, Planning, Execution, Memory or
Evaluation.

---

## 2. Domain Vision

The Self Improvement domain is positioned after the Autonomy Layer and before
future Decision, Planning or Routing influence.

Primary flow:

```text
LearningSignal
|
v
ImprovementProposal
|
v
ImprovementEvaluation
|
v
ImprovementPolicy
|
v
ImprovementDecision
|
v
ImprovementTrace
|
v
ImprovementInfluence
|
v
Future Decision / Planning / Routing
```

The domain transforms learning into governed improvement decisions.

Raw learning signals must never become direct behavioral changes.

## 4. ImprovementProposal

## Purpose

Represents a structured improvement proposal derived from one or more V1.16
LearningSignals.

A proposal is a candidate improvement. It is not an applied change.

---

## Responsibilities

ImprovementProposal must:

- reference one or more source learning signals;
- describe the proposed improvement;
- identify the target layer;
- identify the target component;
- describe the expected benefit;
- estimate initial risk;
- preserve evidence;
- provide traceability metadata.

## 6. ImprovementPolicy

## Purpose

ImprovementPolicy is a governance and configuration layer.

It defines the rules used by the Self Improvement domain to approve, reject or
hold evaluated proposals for review.

ImprovementPolicy is not a behavioral entity and does not execute actions.

---

## Responsibilities

ImprovementPolicy must:

- define confidence thresholds;
- define risk limits;
- define allowed target layers;
- define approval requirements;
- reject unsafe improvement proposals;
- prevent responsibility boundary violations;
- enforce future-only influence.

---

## Attributes

```text
ImprovementPolicy

- policy_id
- minimum_confidence
- maximum_auto_approval_risk
- allowed_target_layers
- approval_required_risk_levels
- allow_future_influence_only
- created_at
- metadata
```

---

## Governance Rules

Initial governance rules:

```text
- Low-confidence proposals must be rejected.
- High-risk proposals must require review.
- Self Improvement cannot execute tools.
- Self Improvement cannot alter original data.
- Self Improvement cannot mutate active execution.
- Raw LearningSignals cannot directly influence Decision or Planning.
- Approved improvements can influence only future behavior.
```

---

## 13. Domain Invariants

The Self Improvement domain must preserve the following invariants:

- Every ImprovementProposal must reference at least one LearningSignal.
- One ImprovementProposal may be derived from multiple LearningSignals.
- No ImprovementProposal can apply behavior directly.
- No ImprovementEvaluation can exist without an ImprovementProposal.
- No ImprovementDecision can exist without an ImprovementEvaluation.
- No ImprovementInfluence can exist without an APPROVED or EXPERIMENTAL decision.
- REJECTED decisions cannot generate active influence.
- PENDING_REVIEW decisions cannot generate active influence.
- ImprovementInfluence must affect only future behavior.
- Self Improvement cannot execute tools.
- Self Improvement cannot mutate active execution state.
- Self Improvement cannot alter original data.
- Raw LearningSignals cannot be consumed directly by Decision or Planning.
- Memory stores improvement records but does not evaluate or approve them.
- Evaluation measures outcomes but does not approve improvements.
- Decision and Planning must remain functional without improvement influence.
- V1.16 autonomy contracts must remain backward compatible.

V1.16 contracts remain valid:

```text
AutonomyContext
Observation
ReflectionResult
LearningSignal
AdaptationStrategy
AutonomyDecision
AutonomyMemory
AutonomyEngine
```

Integration point:

```text
AutonomyEngine
|
v
LearningSignal
|
v
Self Improvement Domain
```

The Self Improvement domain consumes learning signals and may read autonomy
memory, but it does not replace AutonomyEngine, AutonomyDecision or
AutonomyMemory.

## 16. Architectural Separation

## Autonomy

Owns:

- observation;
- reflection;
- learning signal extraction;
- adaptation strategy proposal;
- autonomy decision creation.

Does not own improvement governance.

## Self Improvement

Owns:

- proposal generation;
- evaluation of proposals;
- policy enforcement;
- improvement decisions;
- traces;
- controlled future influence.

Does not execute tools or rewrite active plans.

## Decision

Owns:

- alternative generation;
- alternative evaluation;
- selection;
- confidence analysis;
- explanation.

May consume approved ImprovementInfluence.

Must not consume raw LearningSignals.

## Planning

Owns:

- execution plan creation;
- goal-driven planning;
- dynamic planning;
- replanning strategy.

May consume approved ImprovementInfluence.

Must not consume raw LearningSignals.

## Execution

Owns:

- tool execution;
- execution state;
- execution events;
- execution feedback;
- execution lifecycle.

Must not be directly controlled by Self Improvement.

## Memory

Owns:

- storage;
- retrieval;
- history;
- audit support.

Does not evaluate or approve improvements.

## Evaluation

Owns:

- outcome measurement;
- before-and-after analysis;
- regression identification;
- improvement result validation.

Does not approve improvements.

---

## 17. Domain Summary

The V1.17 Self Improvement domain formalizes a governed path from learning to
future behavior influence.

The central rule is:

```text
Learning is evidence, not behavior.
```

Only evaluated and approved improvements may produce controlled future
influence, and every step must remain traceable, testable and compatible with
the V1.16 autonomy architecture.
