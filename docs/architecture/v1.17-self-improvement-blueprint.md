# V1.17 - Self Improvement Expansion Blueprint

## 1. Objective

The V1.17 Self Improvement Expansion introduces a controlled improvement cycle
on top of the autonomy foundations delivered in V1.16.

The capability objective is to allow the system to transform operational
learning into structured, evaluated and traceable improvement decisions that
can influence future behavior without allowing uncontrolled self-modification.

The V1.17 capability must enable the system to:

- identify improvement opportunities from execution history, metrics and
  failures;
- transform learning signals into structured improvement proposals;
- evaluate the impact, risk and confidence of proposed improvements;
- approve, reject or hold improvements for human review;
- expose approved improvements as controlled influence signals;
- preserve complete traceability across learning, proposal, evaluation,
  decision and future influence;
- validate whether an approved improvement produced measurable benefit.

The architectural focus is governed self-improvement, not unrestricted
autonomous behavior mutation.

---

## 2. V1.16 Context And V1.17 Evolution

V1.16 introduced the Autonomy Layer and established the first explicit cycle for
agent self-observation and behavioral learning.

The V1.16 flow is:

```text
Execution
|
v
Observation
|
v
Reflection
|
v
Learning Signal
|
v
Adaptation Strategy
|
v
Autonomy Decision
|
v
Memory
```

This gives the system the ability to observe execution results, reflect on
outcomes, extract learning signals and propose adaptation strategies.

However, V1.16 is primarily a foundation for autonomy. It records and proposes
behavioral adaptation, but it does not yet define a complete governance model
for converting learning into future behavioral influence.

V1.17 evolves this foundation by adding an explicit Self Improvement Layer:

```text
Learning Signal
|
v
Improvement Proposal
|
v
Improvement Evaluation
|
v
Improvement Decision
|
v
Controlled Influence
|
v
Future Decision / Planning / Routing
```

The key architectural change is that learning no longer moves directly toward
behavioral adaptation. It must pass through proposal, evaluation, policy and
traceability boundaries before influencing future agent behavior.

## 3. New Components

The V1.17 capability introduces a new self-improvement domain.

Suggested module:

```text
src/agents/self_improvement/

|- improvement_proposal.py
|- improvement_evaluation.py
|- improvement_decision.py
|- improvement_policy.py
|- improvement_engine.py
|- improvement_memory.py
|- improvement_trace.py
|- improvement_influence.py
`- __init__.py
```

### 3.1 ImprovementProposal

Represents a structured improvement proposal derived from one or more learning
signals.

### 3.2 ImprovementEvaluation

Represents the evaluation result for an improvement proposal, including
evidence strength, risk, expected impact and confidence.

### 3.3 ImprovementDecision

Represents the governance decision for an improvement proposal.

### 3.4 ImprovementPolicy

Defines the rules used to approve, reject or hold improvement proposals for
manual review.

### 3.5 ImprovementEngine

Coordinates the self-improvement lifecycle.

### 3.6 ImprovementMemory

Stores proposals, evaluations, decisions and related metadata.

### 3.7 ImprovementTrace

Provides auditability across the complete self-improvement lifecycle.

### 3.8 ImprovementInfluence

Represents an approved, explicit and bounded signal that may influence future
decision, planning or routing behavior.

## 4. Responsibilities

## 4.1 ImprovementProposal

Responsibilities:

- describe the proposed improvement;
- identify source learning signals;
- identify the target layer and component;
- record evidence used to justify the proposal;
- define expected benefit;
- define initial risk level;
- preserve metadata for traceability.

The proposal must not apply any behavioral change.

## 4.2 ImprovementEvaluation

Responsibilities:

- evaluate evidence quality;
- evaluate expected impact;
- evaluate risk;
- validate architectural compatibility;
- provide recommendations;
- produce an approval recommendation.

The evaluation must not mutate runtime behavior.

## 4.3 ImprovementDecision

Responsibilities:

- approve, reject or hold a proposal for review;
- record the reason for the decision;
- indicate whether human approval is required;
- preserve confidence and governance metadata;
- provide an auditable decision boundary before future influence is allowed.

## 4.4 ImprovementPolicy

Responsibilities:

- define minimum confidence thresholds;
- define risk rules;
- define approval requirements;
- define allowed target layers;
- prevent critical changes without explicit approval;
- prevent self-improvement from executing tools or modifying original data.

Example policy constraints:

```text
confidence >= 0.75 for automatic approval
HIGH risk requires human approval
Self Improvement cannot execute tools
Self Improvement cannot alter original data
Approved improvements only influence future executions
```

## 4.5 ImprovementEngine

Responsibilities:

- consume learning signals from the autonomy layer;
- generate improvement proposals;
- evaluate proposals;
- apply improvement policy;
- create improvement decisions;
- persist improvement traces;
- expose approved improvements as controlled influence signals.

The engine coordinates the cycle but does not execute tools, rewrite plans
directly or mutate decision components implicitly.

## 4.6 ImprovementMemory

Responsibilities:

- store improvement proposals;
- store improvement evaluations;
- store improvement decisions;
- store improvement traces;
- support retrieval by proposal id, decision id, target layer, target component
  and status;
- preserve rejected and pending improvements for auditability.

## 4.7 ImprovementTrace

Responsibilities:

- connect learning signals to proposals;
- connect proposals to evaluations;
- connect evaluations to decisions;
- connect decisions to controlled influence signals;
- support debugging, audit and validation of improvement outcomes.

## 4.8 ImprovementInfluence

Responsibilities:

- represent an approved future influence;
- describe the target layer and component;
- define influence type and weight;
- preserve confidence and reason;
- optionally expire or become inactive after a defined scope.

Influence is not execution. It is an explicit input to future decision, routing
or planning behavior.

## 7. Impact On Existing Architecture

V1.17 primarily impacts the transition between:

```text
Autonomy Layer -> Memory Layer -> Decision / Planning / Routing
```

### 7.1 Autonomy Layer

The Autonomy Layer remains responsible for:

- observation;
- reflection;
- learning signal generation;
- adaptation strategy generation;
- autonomy decisions.

It should not become responsible for improvement governance.

### 7.2 Self Improvement Layer

The new layer becomes responsible for:

- converting learning signals into improvement proposals;
- evaluating and governing proposed improvements;
- producing controlled future influence signals.

### 7.3 Memory Layer

The Memory Layer gains a richer improvement history:

- proposals;
- evaluations;
- decisions;
- traces;
- future influence records.

Memory should preserve both accepted and rejected improvements.

### 7.4 Decision Layer

The Decision Layer may consume approved improvement influence signals.

It must continue to work without self-improvement input, preserving backward
compatibility.

### 7.5 Planning System

The Planning System may receive approved improvement influence, especially for
strategy selection and replanning preferences.

Planning must not depend on raw learning signals.

### 7.6 Execution Engine

The Execution Engine should not be directly modified by self-improvement.

Self-improvement must not execute tools or mutate in-flight execution state.

### 7.7 Evaluation Layer

The Evaluation Layer becomes more important for measuring whether approved
improvements produced measurable benefit.

It should support before-and-after validation for improvement outcomes.

## 8. Required ADRs

### ADR-017-001 - Self Improvement Layer

Defines the new Self Improvement Layer and its position after Autonomy and
before controlled future influence.

### ADR-017-002 - Improvement Governance Policy

Defines confidence thresholds, risk levels, approval requirements and allowed
target layers.

### ADR-017-003 - Improvement Memory And Traceability

Defines how proposals, evaluations, decisions, traces and influence signals are
stored and retrieved.

### ADR-017-004 - Controlled Influence On Decision And Planning

Defines how approved improvements may influence Decision, Planning and Routing
without breaking existing contracts.

### ADR-017-005 - Self Improvement Validation Strategy

Defines validation metrics, before-and-after comparison, regression checks and
rollback semantics.

## 9. Test Strategy

### 9.1 Unit Tests

Unit tests must cover:

- `ImprovementProposal` creation;
- `ImprovementEvaluation` creation;
- `ImprovementDecision` creation;
- `ImprovementTrace` creation;
- `ImprovementInfluence` creation;
- policy approval by confidence threshold;
- policy rejection by low confidence;
- policy review requirement for high risk;
- memory storage and retrieval;
- trace creation across the full lifecycle.

### 9.2 Integration Tests

Integration tests must cover:

- `LearningSignal` generating an `ImprovementProposal`;
- proposal evaluation;
- policy decision;
- approved decision stored in memory;
- rejected decision stored without future influence;
- approved decision producing `ImprovementInfluence`;
- Decision Layer behavior with and without improvement influence;
- Planning behavior with and without improvement influence;
- Routing behavior with and without improvement influence.

### 9.3 Regression Tests

Regression tests must guarantee:

- V1.16 autonomy contracts still work;
- `AutonomyEngine.evaluate_execution()` remains backward compatible;
- existing runtime flows do not require self-improvement components;
- existing decision, planning and execution tests continue to pass;
- no existing tests are removed.

### 9.4 Behavioral Tests

Behavioral tests should validate:

- repeated failures generate improvement proposals;
- consistent successes can reinforce future strategy selection;
- low-evidence proposals are rejected;
- high-risk proposals require review;
- approved improvements affect only future decisions;
- measurable before-and-after improvement can be recorded.

---

## 10. Release Acceptance Criteria

V1.17 is accepted when:

- a distinct Self Improvement Layer exists;
- self-improvement responsibilities are separated from Autonomy, Decision,
  Planning and Execution;
- every improvement has proposal, evaluation, decision and trace records;
- low-confidence improvements are rejected;
- high-risk improvements require approval;
- approved improvements generate explicit controlled influence signals;
- controlled influence affects only future executions;
- self-improvement does not execute tools directly;
- self-improvement does not alter original data;
- V1.16 contracts remain backward compatible;
- documentation and ADRs are updated;
- unit, integration, regression and behavioral tests validate the full flow;
- a complete release demonstration can show:

```text
Execution failure
|
v
Observation
|
v
LearningSignal
|
v
ImprovementProposal
|
v
ImprovementEvaluation
|
v
ImprovementDecision
|
v
ImprovementTrace
|
v
Controlled Future Influence
```

The release goal is to make the system capable of learning from its own
operation while keeping improvement explicit, governed, testable and auditable.
